Pre‑Export Finance — Flow

  1. An export, purchase or equivalent contract is signed between the local Producer and the Offtaker and subsequently assigned to the Investors. Payment by the Offtaker is credited directly to the Investors.
  2. Investors disburse the loan in USD to the Producer. Such financing is reported to the Central Bank.
  3. Through a Local Bank, the Producer converts the USD into local currency to operate.
  4. Once the product is exported, the Offtaker pays the sale proceeds directly to the Investors’ account abroad.

Import Finance — Flow

  1. The Central Bank enables the Investor’s disbursement for import payments to the Supplier to be made directly abroad (cash or in advance against import documents).
  2. The Producer operates normally, complying with Import Payment Monitoring through a local Financial Institution.
  3. At the agreed maturity, the Central Bank authorizes the Producer to transfer USD abroad to the Investors’ account through a local Financial Institution.

Investment Finance

  • Export‑oriented Project Finance / CapEx tailored to the project’s needs.
  • Structures aligned with Central Bank standards, enabling efficient medium/long‑term financing with repayment abroad.
  • Debt refinancing, applying export revenues abroad to optimize the balance sheet.
